How to Use an Architect Ruler in ¼” Scale:

Try it Yourself

Follow along with this lesson to become more comfortable with the architect ruler.

Scroll down to see all four steps.

  • Step 1

    Find the part of the ruler that reads ¼”. This will be the only part of the ruler you will use this semester. It may appear backward or upside down to you as there are so many options on the ruler on all sides and ends.

  • Step 2

    Lay the ruler on your graph paper so that the 0 is on a blue line.

    0 will ALWAYS be the starting point to measure.

    On the other side of the 0 to the end of the ruler you will see several small and close lines that also fill one square. These are the inches in a foot. There are 12 inches in a foot. The longest line in the middle of the inches is the 6” mark.

  • Step 3

    From the 0 across the length of the ruler you will see even numbers of 2, 4, 6 etc. These are feet.

  • Step 4

    If you draw a room that is in feet only you can count the numbers to match the length of the room like a room that is 10 feet long, you would look over the number 10 and draw a line from 0-10 = 10 feet. If the room was 10 foot 4 inches…you would draw a line from the 0-10 and then from the other side of the 0 count over 4 of the little lines to = 4 inches. A room that is 10 foot, 4 inches can be written 10’4”.

IDEC1055 Final Project

Due: By the last day of the week (11:55 p.m.) of Week 13
Submit to: Final Project Submission Folder
Value of assignment: 35% of your final grade
Evaluation: Final Project Rubric

Rationale

  • Apply the techniques of dimensioning and elevating to scale
  • Develop floor plan layouts for various rooms of the house
  • Identify conditions and limitations involved in space planning, including standard rooms and furniture dimensions
  • Outline sample gathering processes to prepare for presentation to clients
  • Establish adequate practices to create presentation boards

Overview

This final project includes 3 design boards (2 drawing boards with elevations, textures and electrical overlays, 1 sample board to include both rooms on 1 board), an essay of 1,000 words or more and a video presentation describing their design.

Instructions

As the decorator, you are to design 2 rooms for an imaginary "client" placed on a total of 3 presentation boards.

Select 2 of the following rooms:

  1. Board 1 — Living room or Family room
  2. Board 2 — Bedroom or Kitchen

Specifications

  • Determine who the client is, including # of people using the space, style, colour scheme, lifestyle, uses of space etc.
  • Do not use rooms, colour or styles already used in previous final projects
  • Using what has been learned throughout the semester; design an ideal space for the client.
  • Floor plans and elevations on presentation boards as well as providing samples of materials used such as flooring, window treatments, furniture, bedding, cabinets, counters etc. as per room selected will be required
  • All selections must be real (furniture). You are expected to research actual sizes of furniture, cabinets etc. Samples are your choice of texture and colour
  • Information such as your name, room title, scale, direction arrow placed together on right side of board
  • Board will be graded on neatness, impression of board design and layout, accuracy, clear understanding of plan, accurate style interpretation

Project Board Fronts

  • 3 Boards total
  • Each Board size — 1/2 sheet of foam board, white or black only, measuring 15” x 20” all white or black for 3 boards
  • Must have a minimum ½” real border or implied border
  • All mounts and printing to be in black or white only

Board 1 & 2 — Drawing of Rooms with Elevations

  • Accurate ¼” scale drawing on vellum, in black, permanent pigment liner (draw first on graph paper and include all working graph paper pages in essay portion of the assignment)
  • Rooms of student choice minimum 11’ x 18’ in size for Living room or Family room on board #1
  • Room of student choice minimum 11’ x 18 in size for Bedroom or Kitchen on board #2
  • 1 room to be fold out elevation style and the 2nd room to be separate elevation style
  • Furnish each room with appropriate items on floor plan
  • Elevations: detail on anything on the wall and furniture that is up against the wall (fireplace, wall units, tables, upholstery, lamps, plants, beds etc.) or within 1’ of the wall
  • 1 room must have either a bookcase or fireplace
  • Include texture for all floor plan drawings and elevations (floor, walls, window treatment etc.)
  • Add the room’s legend on each drawing board. Example: Living room board &ndas; Living room legend. Kitchen board – Kitchen legend
  • The sample board does NOT have a legend. See explanation below
  • Furniture labelled by letter or number to relate to legend and samples
  • 1 legend only per room. Item in drawing #1, sample #1 etc. If using #’s for board 1, continue using #’s for board 2 where you left off from board 1
  • DO NOT USE THE SAME # OR LETTER ON BOTH BOARDS
  • Add electrical overlay on each drawing board
  • Dimensions of the floor plan and elevations

Sample Board

  • Samples on board that show upholstery, fabrics, window treatments, flooring, wall colour (paint or wallpaper, trim) or other materials that show the client the decorator’s ideas for the space
  • Photographs are not necessary, however if used to be maximum of 2 for the entire board. Mounted on Bristol board in opposite colour of presentation board
  • Place layout of board to make interesting presentation, filling the board and not leaving too much negative space.

Essay Instructions

Write a detailed essay typed on white letter size paper using black lettering only. Arial or Calibri font size 12, single spaced. 1000 words minimum.

See "Tips for Writing Essays"

Title page (1 page)

Student name, Course title, date, project title

Table of Contents (1 page)

Stating what pages are in essay in order and page numbers.

Introduction (1 Page)

Who the room is for (client) – lifestyle, age, and career and any other information you want the reader to know about the client.

Describe style chosen. If it is an eclectic mix state the styles combined in the room.

Mood desired and how the room is to be used.

Body of information (2-3 Pages)

  • Paragraphs that clearly explain and support the design board created
  • 1 topic per paragraph. NO titles
  • Use proper terminology and technical terms in describing the design and items
  • Do not use words such as “pretty, nice and beautiful” as they are subjective and not technical words
  • Do not repeat material. Put all detail about an item in 1 spot. Example: sofa = visual description, colour, fabric etc. all in same spot.
  • Be careful of plagiarism. Must be written in the students own words
  • Do not repeat lesson notes. This is filler and is not considered part of the essay requirements
  • Typed on white paper with black lettering only on letter size pages. Single line spacing
  • Proper grammar and spelling (Canadian English). Use spell check.
  • Review, reading out loud or with another person to make sure the essay makes sense and is easy to understand. The description should be detailed enough that if someone was listening to it could get a clear mental picture of the look, shape, style and colour of each item.
  • Review that the essay requirements have been fulfilled
  • Photographs are not to be included in essay

Conclusion (1 Page)

Supporting the reasons for selections and placement. Example: how the client is happy with the design or how the principles and elements have come together in the design to a pleasing result.

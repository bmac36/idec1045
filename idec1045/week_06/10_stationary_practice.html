Practice: Stationary Side Panels

Determine the amount of fabric and lining required as well as length of the decorative rod for the following:

  • Window 55” wide x 66” long + add 3” trim to outside of this window
  • 9’ ceiling
  • Ceiling to window = 20” glass
  • Pair of stationary side panels with 27” pattern repeat
  • Pleated @ 2 ½ fullness
  • Each panel side to cover 12” of wall past the trim on each side
  • Hang ceiling to floor on an I beam
  • What is yardage of fabric and lining
  • How wide should the rod be?

Rod needed:

If you want 12” of treatment on the wall and window is 55” wide:

  • Panel = 18” – 12” on wall – 3” trim = 3” over window
  • Window glass = 55” – 3” – 3” (over window) = 49” exposed glass + 18” + 18” (width of panels)= 85” rod = 7.08’ rod or 7’ rod
  • You can round down because rod size is less than .1 over the size. With a stationary treatment you can place the panels anywhere you want on the wall so you are able to round down

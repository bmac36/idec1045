Assignment 3: Shade and Valance Calculations

Due: By the last day of the week (11:59 p.m.) of Week 8
Submit to: Shade and Valance Calculations Dropbox
Value of assignment: 10% of your final grade
Evaluation: Shade and Valance Calculations Rubric

Rationale

  • Outline current fabric trends including pattern, texture and styles as well as uses and benefits of linings to create quality window treatments for various uses
  • Calculate both hard and soft treatments to custom order from a manufacturer or seamstress based on appropriate measurements to complete a window treatment order for a client

Overview

This assignment gives the student practice to calculate and plan valances and shades that they will also apply in the mid-term, exam and final project for this course.

Instructions

  • Use one page of graph paper for each window
  • Draw the window as your working copy in ½ inch scale
  • Calculate the scenarios listed below, explaining in detail how you came to each amount step by step.
  • All trim is 3 inches wide around the window measurements given below.
  • Label each page by the question number.

Scenarios:

  1. Calculate the fabric, interlining and lining required for a flat roman shade. It is an inside mount on a window that is 48 inches wide and 60 inches long. Plain fabric hung on a Velcro board.
  2. Calculate the fabric required for an outside mount mock roman valance that has 3 folds. The treatment is hung 6 inches above the trim and covers the top of the window glass by 4 inches. The treatment goes past the window trim by 4 inches on each side and has 4 inch returns. The window is 70 inches wide by 70 inches long.
  3. Calculate the fabric, interlining and lining required for a flat roller shade, outside mount hung 4 inches above the upper trim and covering 2 inches below the bottom trim when closed. It is hung 2 inches past the sides of the trim width. The fabric has a 20 inch pattern repeat. The window glass is 75 inches wide and 72 inches long.
  4. Calculate the fabric interlining and lining required for a box pleat valance, outside mount that is past the trim by 8 inches in each side with 4 inch returns. There are 4 full pleats across the window and ½ pleats at the corners. There is a 16 inch pattern repeat. The valance is hung at the ceiling height on a ceiling is 10 inches above the window trim and the valance covers 4 inches of the window glass. The window is 72 inches wide and 65 inches long.

Notes

  • Post all pages into a word document and place in the dropbox by the end of week 8.

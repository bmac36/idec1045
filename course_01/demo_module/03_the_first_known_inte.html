The First Known Interior Decorator in North America

The first person to call herself an “Interior Decorator” was Elsie DeWolfe circa 1859 – 1950

Early in the 20th century she took the current Victorian design and altered it to more individual tastes. She often would lighten all the wood in a room with paint to take away from the heavy feeling of a Victorian room. She used furniture from various countries in her designs.

She hired trades to paint or wall paper rooms, added accessories etc. to create a more individual look. She referred to her wealthy clients as “Ladies of Good taste” and wrote a book called “House of Good Taste” in 1913. This book is all about the Principles and Elements of design. The average person could not afford to hire a decorator and were not exposed to this new kind of decorating. At this time only wealthy people could afford a decorator and it was a high end industry. For many decades, hiring a professional decorator was only for the wealthy and not the average home.

Overall, good design has not changed. Style and colour trends change but good design is still the same Principles and Elements that we will learn in detail next week.

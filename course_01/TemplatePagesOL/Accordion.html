Chandelier

Chandeliers are hang from a cord or chain from the ceiling and come in many styles, sizes and finishes. They are used in any room of the home. Styles run from traditional metal and crystal to contemporary drum looks. They look great over a coffee table or a small one between chairs, as night stand lights, in bathrooms for an elegant effect and of course in hallways and dining rooms. You will even see them over a kitchen island.

Recess Pot lights or track light

This kind of lighting is becoming a staple in new home builds and renovations and are smaller than ones used in the past measuring about 4” in diameter. All overhead lighting should be on dimmers to adjust the light as needed. It is best to NOT place in a grid pattern to cover the entire room but use in areas where you want to emphasize something. For example, over a wall of art, in corners of a dining, or small room to make it feel larger.

Recess lights are not just for the ceiling. Use them on the wall at floor height or recessed in the floor for a dramatic effect. They are very functional for staircases for safe travel on the stairway.

Track lights come in many styles from wire or rod rails that can attach many different fixtures to adapt to the needs. They come in many colours, finishes and styles to suit all styles of décor. Track lights look great on a wall too!

Buffet Lamps

Buffet lamps are accent lamps used to create low relaxing light.

They are fun with varying kinds of shades and bases, usually used in pairs on sofa tables behind a sofa, consoles in halls, low dressers in bedrooms, buffets in dining rooms or fireplace mantels for accent. Common wattage is from 25 – 60 watt of incandescent light.

Halogen bulbs

These are bright white bulbs used mostly in kitchen and dressing areas that are good for task areas and will show a truer colour. Make sure to get the low voltage ones as the others can get very hot to stand under.

Natural light

Natural light and large windows are a huge trend. New construction often use 2 story windows and ceiling to floor windows are common in condos that flood light into the home.

Wall washing

Wall washing is a great technique to “wash” over the wall area to accent drapery, wall art or an area you want to highlight. This technique is achieved by placing recess pot or track lights close to the wall to shine on a wall surface.

Indirect lighting

Indirect light is when the shade is on the bottom casting light upward. It is a softer kind of light and is used in some chandeliers over tables or wall lights in bathrooms to soften light. Make sure to clean inside the shade often for dust and bugs that may land inside.

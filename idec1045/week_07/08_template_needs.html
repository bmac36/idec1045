Template Needs

When to Use a Template

Sometimes you will come across an unusual window that the client wants covered. Arches, circles, Quarter circles are architectural in nature and not meant to be covered.

Rarely are arched windows perfectly proportioned and not a perfect semi-circle. For example, a perfect semi-circle would measure is 60 inches wide by 30 inches high.

Since most are not perfect, if you do have this request it must be planned with a template as it is the only way to get an exact fit.

Stepst to make a template

  1. Tape butcher paper or brown wrapping paper over the window opening. The paper must be strong enough to withstand the handling in the window.
  2. Allow plenty of overlap and use paper that has never been folded or creased.
  3. Draw the exact outline of the window by tracing over the inside edge of the window frame.
  4. If using an outside mount blind add the desired amount past the frame and draw a second outline around it for the size of the finished shade.
  5. Add all measurements on the template to send to the manufacturer.

Space planning rule list

Resource

Now that you’ve had a chance to review some of the space planning rules, here they all are in a convenient document for you.

You can download a copy of the rule list as a PDF download pdf document

  1. When a room is longer than 12-14’, you must treat the room in more than one area.
  2. Every room must have a focal point. If there is not an obvious one in the room, then one must be created.
  3. Each person in the room must have a table within reach.
  4. Place furniture close together… about 6”-8” apart.
  5. When furniture is placed at right angles the best shape of end table is a round or square placed about 6” away from the furniture.
  6. When placing upholstery against the wall, keep it out from the wall at least 6” and NOT right against the wall.
  7. There must be 2½” – 3’ minimum for a traffic area to walk around a room and enter a furniture grouping.
  8. The distance between a coffee or cocktail table and the edge of a seat is to be between 16” minimum – 24” maximum.
  9. Chandeliers must be centered over the dining table.
  10. The traffic space needed around a dining table and the next thing it hits (buffet, wall etc.) must be a minimum of 3’.
  11. When adding an area rug under a dining table it must be 2’ larger on all 4 sides of the table.
  12. Upholstery can be put in front of a window without blocking the light.
  13. Think about using different sizes or kinds of furniture to best suit the needs.
  14. Upholstery should not be placed right into a corner or wall but a minimum of 2’ out from the wall or corner.
  15. Create one of the 4 basic furniture arrangements to create comfortable conversation groups. These are L, C/U, Parallel or circular.

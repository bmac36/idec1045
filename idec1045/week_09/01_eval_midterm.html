Midterm Assignment

Due: By the last day of the week (11:59 p.m.) of Week 7
Submit to:Mid-term Submission Folder
Value of assignment: 10% of your final grade
Evaluation: Midterm Rubric

Rationale

  • Identify conditions and limitations involved in space planning, including standard rooms and furniture dimensions.
  • Outline sample gathering processes to prepare for presentation to clients.

Overview

This is a drawing assignment including an electrical overlay for 2 rooms for an imaginary client. The student will also include a written portion explaining in detail the design and furniture etc. included in each room

This midterm is a continuum of previous lessons testing space planning skills, drawing skills and electrical overlay application to a drawing.

Instructions

Mr. & Mrs. Jones have hired you to decorate their bedrooms in their condo in the heart of the city. They are in their 30’s with no children. They are looking for a plan for their master bedroom and guest/office room.

1. Master Bedroom – Room size = 13'x19'

  • Closet drawn outside room dimensions: size/style you prefer
  • Doors & windows in any position you prefer
  • Client likes to read and watch TV in bedroom in evenings
  • Like many styles and are looking for suggestions to create an elegant but relaxing feeling in this space

2. Office/Guest Room – Room Size = 13'x14'

  • Closet drawn outside room dimensions
  • Doors & windows in any position you prefer
  • Need space for office as client works often from home
  • Need space for occasional 2 guests (4-5 times per year)
  • Want to maximize storage
  • Client looking to decorator for colour and style suggestions and way to combine both room needs

Note: Floor Plan and electrical legend only: No elevations or texture

  • Use separate page of graph paper (letter size graph paper is large enough for this assignment) for each room

Drawing for each room:

  • Using ¼” scale, draw rooms on graph paper in pencil (shade walls)
  • Add measurements and breakdowns around all 4 walls of each room
  • Add your name, scale and direction arrow on the right side of the graph paper
  • Add furniture, window treatments, flooring etc. As needed for each space
  • Add legend identifying furniture on your floor plan on the right side of the graph paper
  • Create an electrical overlay for each room on acetate using the acetate permanent pigment liner
  • Tape electrical overlay on graph paper using double sided tape across the top only of your drawing
  • Add electrical legend to the right side of the graph paper under the furniture legend

Written portion for each room:

  • Select a style of your choice
  • In point form describe style, colours, flooring, furniture and accessories so that the reader can get a clear vision of how the rooms looks
  • May refer to text and class notes

Notes

  • Post in the Midterm dropbox as two (2) documents:
    • One (1) document for the Master bedroom drawing and written portion
    • One (1) document for the Office/guest room drawing and written portion

Enjoy and let your decorating ideas abound!

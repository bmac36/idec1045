Spatial Illusion

It is important to consider the size of the space to determine what kind of furnishings, colours, textures, patterns and visual impact will best enhance the room. To do this, decorators use several methods to make spaces feel comfortable.

Make small rooms feel big

When planning small spaces, the goal is to create the illusion of a larger area and not feel claustrophobic.

This is done in the following ways…

  • 1. If an area is small, it is important to allow the eye to move easily beyond the immediate space you are in by the use of uncluttered openings or being able to see into adjacent rooms or even to the outdoors. This can be done with open plans or bigger openings between rooms. Diagonal lines can help with this movement or having large uncovered windows that allow you to see outside. If you can see beyond the room, it gives the illusion of a much larger space.

  • 2. Placing larger pieces of furniture parallel to the walls creates more floor space visible and therefore a feeling of space. Placing furniture around the walls does not divide the space into smaller segments and opens up more floor space.

  • 3. Small rooms can be made to feel larger by the use of smaller scale furniture, textures and patterns. When carefully choosing furniture that may be able to serve two purposes, (like an end table that can also be used for storage), make sure that the room does not get filled with too many furniture items. Using fewer accessories also gives the illusion of space .

  • 4. Empty, silent spaces are important in a small room. The use of negative space allows the eye to rest and see between furniture and on walls. “Less is more” is the idea that one does not need to fill a space with furniture just because it is there.

  • 5. Expose as much of the floor area as possible by using furniture that sits on legs up off the floor. When able to see more floor space by using glass tables or transparent pieces it creates the feeling of more room. It is not necessary to have all furniture on tall legs, however in a small space some pieces are helpful.

  • 6.The use of mirrors and trompe l’oeil effects are great ways to create the illusion of depth. Mirrors can be a decorator’s best friend. Especially when used in large pieces, they can double the visual size of a room. Consider using a large mirror on a wall or leaning on the floor. Using plain mirror panels in 4’x8’ sheets on a wall can make any small room feel much larger. Trompe l’oeil is a French word meaning “trick the eye”. They can be found in murals, wallpaper scenes, and large art that looks like one is outside. They act as a window to see beyond the space.

  • 7. Unify the space as much as possible. Using wall to wall floor coverings, low contrast between different floors, floor to ceiling window treatments, and blending colours as much as possible will create an illusion of a larger area. In a small space if the furniture is close to the wall colour it will blend into the wall, thus making the space feel larger.

  • 8. Selecting light and cool colours will seem to expand the space. Cool colours recede in a room and make a space feel larger. Light colours or colours that blend as in the photo enhance this feeling

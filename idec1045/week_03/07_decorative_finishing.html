Decorative Finishing

Let's look at the second method of finishing: Decorative

Decorative finishing gives the fabric a specific decorative look or improves the feel or appearance of a fabric.

Select each item for more information.

Printing

Printing is one of the most common treatments that add a pattern to a plain cloth after weaving. Some cloths are woven in a pattern and others have the print applied to a plain cloth. While most printing is applied in this way, some fabrics can have a print woven into the fabric from the yarn stage during the weaving stage. Applied pattern prints can go in and out of style by the kind of print, colour or even size of the pattern.

Embossing

Embossing is the use of an engraved roller to create a permanent 3D effect

Etching or Burnout

Etching or burnout applies an acidic compound that burns or etches a fiber to reveal some sheer pattern in a fabric.

Flocking

Flocking is a decorative pattern made with small fibers bonded to a fabric.

There are many more kinds of techniques on the market such as heat set, lappet, embroidered, plisse, moiré and more. Doing some research into the world of fabrics can allow you to discover some of these other interesting decorative ways to make fabric.

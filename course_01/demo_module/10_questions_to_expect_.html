Questions to Expect from a Decorator

The following questions are important to know about a client in order to begin a job for them. It is not something that you ask your client one at a time in one sitting, however it is a list of questions you need to find out about through your first meeting at their home. The answers will give you a better idea of what your client is wanting and so you can get to know them better.

  1. Name, Address
  2. Rent or own?
  3. Number of adults and children in the home?
  4. What are the ages of the children?
  5. What is the age of home and style (size, 1 or multiple level, open or closed concept)?
  6. How long have they lived there?
  7. What style does the client like (Traditional, country, contemporary etc.)?
  8. Preferred textures (rough, smooth, leather etc.)
  9. What mood is wanted for the space (lively, fun, relaxed, formal, informal)?
  10. What is being discarded?
  11. What has to stay and need to work around?
  12. What new is being added?
  13. What is on floor (carpet, wood, tile etc.)?
  14. What is on the walls (Paint, wallpaper, panel etc.)?
  15. What window treatments are available? (what is there and what is needed)
  16. What are the room dimensions?
  17. What colour preferences does the client have?
  18. Where are the natural light sources (direction of light, how much light comes in windows)?
  19. Where are the artificial light sources (lamps, ceiling lights)?
  20. What is the focal point of room (Fireplace, TV, wall unit etc.)?
  21. Is there a TV or stereo in the room?
  22. What are the architectural features to consider (pillars, built- ins, sunken rooms, French doors etc.)?
  23. What seating is required, minimum and maximum requirements?
  24. What is the room used for (list activities)?
  25. What accessories are in the room (art, books, games etc.?)
  26. What is their time frame and budget?
  27. What are their long or short term goals with the home?

Try it Yourself:

Try asking a friend or a family member these questions (starting at #7) about a room in home, and their general preferences. It’s fascinating to hear the responses and start to explore ideas!

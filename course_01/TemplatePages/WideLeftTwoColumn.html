Arts and Crafts: 1860-1900

The Arts and Crafts movement was a rebellion to the Victorian era. Craftsmen and furniture makers were not in favour of the mass produced furniture and lesser quality that handmade furniture of previous eras produced. It was a vastly different look to the Victorian era that had lots of curve and detail. Arts and crafts was very modern looking with straight lines made from quarter sawn oak.

This movement was headed by William Morris who went back to handcrafted furniture in simple, cleaner line looks, nature inspired and using repeated patterns in wall paper and fabrics. Following him were several others such as furniture makers in the United States such as Stickley.

Characteristics:

  • Furniture made of quarter sawn oak with exposed peg construction
  • Well-built and sturdy using straight clean lines
  • Square recess doors, wall panels (today called Mission or Shaker as well)
  • Nature inspired colours like green, brown, gold, brick red
  • Use of leather upholstery and wood framed furniture, some built-in
  • Hammered iron hardware and other inherent materials like wood, leather, stone
  • Lighting used to create mood with soft golden glow with glass shades often in an amber colour
  • Accessories like pottery, brass, ceramic, stained glass windows and geometric shapes and forms

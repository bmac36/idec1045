Mid-Term Assignment

Due: By the last day of the week (11:59 p.m.) of Week 9
Submit to: Mid-term Dropbox
Value of assignment: 10% of your final grade
Evaluation: Midterm Rubric

Rationale

  • Explain fabric and drapery terminology, dye lot differences, pattern repeat, railroading and common care codes for cleaning and maintaining materials.
  • Outline current fabric trends including pattern, texture and styles as well as uses and benefits of linings to create quality window treatments for various uses.
  • Calculate both hard and soft treatments to custom order from a manufacturer or seamstress based on appropriate measurements to complete a window treatment order for a client.

Overview

This mid-term is a culmination of all lessons to date where you must decide on a client and design window treatments to suit the client and their style. You are required to calculate all forms of window treatments from stationary to functional drapes, hard treatment, valances and shades. This is excellent review and practice to make sure you understand how to calculate various treatments before the exam and final project are due.

Instructions

DESIGN WINDOW TREATMENTS USING THE INFORMATION PROVIDED

  • You are provided with a floor plan of a home that has 9’ ceilings
  • Trim is 3 inches wide on all windows, not included in window measurements shown
  • On a separate page for each window, draw out the window to ½ inch scale on graph paper in pencil, showing a brief sketch of the treatment used

Create window treatments for 5 window areas

Use the following guidelines in choosing your window treatments:

  • 1 window to be a hard blind treatment
  • 1 treatment to be a valance
  • 1 window to be operating drapery or a layered treatment
  • 1 window to have a pattern repeat of 9”
  • Other windows can be a style of your choice (no treatment is not an option)

Note: The Den is excluded in this assignment

1. Family Room

2 windows flanking fireplace measure 18” off the floor, 36” wide x 60” tall each

  • Space on each side of window = 12” Not including trim (9” from trim to fireplace and corner on each side)

1 large on left side of room. 18” off the floor, 77” wide x 60” tall

DO NOT DO DOOR TO PORCH

2. KITCHEN:

1 window = 36” wide x 36” tall, 24” from the ceiling

  • Window frame touches the cabinets on each side

3. MASTER BEDROOM:

1 window 72” wide x 61” tall

  • Window 18” off the floor
  • Space on each side of window = 36”

4. MASTER BATHROOM:

Window 48” wide x 48” tall

  • 20” from the ceiling
  • Space on each side of window = 4”not including trim (1” including trim)

Drawing for each window treatment:

  • Using ½” scale, draw each window on graph paper in pencil, including a sketch of the treatment used
  • Determine measurements as a group. Work the calculations together so that you all understand how you came to the answers.
  • Label each page by room and treatment style
  • Drawing and calculations on 1 page for each window
  • Written portion can be on the same page or separate page describing calculations and label the same as the drawing associated with it

Written portion for each window treatment:

  • In point form describe your client briefly & style on a separate page from calculations
  • In point form describe the treatment selected for each window, colour, brief fabric description, and how they will be hung (rod/ring diameter or Velcro board etc.)
  • Explain why you selected the treatment for each room area to suit your client’s needs.
  • Calculate the order yardage used for each window area showing the formula used to get the amount in detail so that your instructor can understand your calculations. All fabric width 54”, sheer 118” wide
  • State the finished width and length of each treatment as well as the amount needed to order.
  • If using hard treatment or fabric shade, state which side the operating cord should be located (right or left side).
  • You may determine how high and wide you want the treatment to be
  • How much and type of lining is used for the treatment (if applicable)

Notes

  • Place all pages on a word document and post as one document in the Midterm dropbox provided.
  • You may use your notes as reference
  • You have the entire week to do this assignment
